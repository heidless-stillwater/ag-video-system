# User Journey Map: Your First Documentary

> *Welcome! This guide is designed for complete beginners. If you can write an email, you can make a documentary with this system.*

## The Big Picture: How This Works
Think of this application as a **factory assembly line** for making videos. You don't need to know how the machines work; you just need to hand the factory a topic, and press three buttons in order. 

Here are the 3 most important tasks you need to perform to get your first video.

---

## Task 1: How to Pick Your Subject (The "Draft" Phase)

**Why do I care?** 
Before the factory can build anything, it needs a blueprint. This step tells the artificial intelligence exactly what you want to learn and share with the world.

**How to do it:**
1. Look for the big **"Create New Project"** button on your main dashboard. Click it.
2. You will see a form asking for a **Topic**. Think of this like the title of a book. (Example: *The History of the Lighthouse*).
3. Add a short **Description**. Think of this like the blurb on the back of the book. (Example: *Focus on how they saved ships before electricity was invented.*)
4. Click **"Save"**. 
5. *Success! You have created an empty folder for your project.*

> [!TIP]
> Don't overthink the description. The AI is very smart and will fill in the gaps!

---

## Task 2: How to Send Out the Researchers (The "Intelligence" Phase)

**Why do I care?**
You don't want to spend three days reading Wikipedia. In this step, you are sending a team of robotic librarians out into the internet to fetch verified facts for your video.

**How to do it:**
1. Open your new project. You will see a large button labeled **"Open Stillwater Intelligence"**. Click it.
2. A window will pop up. This is your command center. Look for the **"Choose Persona"** dropdown menu.
3. A **Persona** is like picking the "vibe" of your researcher. If you want historical facts, pick *Historian*. If you want hard data, pick *Scientist*. 
4. Click **"Initiate Research Synthesis"**.
5. *Success! You will see status bars moving. That means the robotic librarians are working.*

> [!CAUTION]
> Do not close your browser tab while the research bars are moving. It usually takes 2-3 minutes for the librarians to return with their notes.

---

## Task 3: How to Turn Facts into a Movie (The "Script & Visualize" Phase)

**Why do I care?**
Right now, you just have a big pile of notes. This final task turns those notes into a spoken script, generates beautiful pictures to match the words, and stitches it all together into a video file you can share.

**How to do it:**
1. Once the research is done, look for the button labeled **"Generate Script"**. Click it. The system will write the voiceover for your documentary.
2. Look over the written words. If they look good, find the **"Visual Style"** dropdown. 
3. This is like picking the camera lens for your movie. *Cinematic* looks like a real movie; *Watercolor* looks like a painting. Pick one!
4. Click **"Generate Visual Assets"**. The system will now paint pictures for every scene in your script.
5. Finally, click **"Assemble and Render"**. This stitches the voice, pictures, and background music together. 
6. *Success! When the progress bar hits 100%, look for the **"Download"** button to save your new movie.*

---

## Simple English Glossary

*   **Assembly Line / Render:** Sticking all the separate parts (audio, video, pictures) together into one final file you can watch on your phone.
*   **Persona:** The "personality" or "lens" the AI uses to look at a topic. 
*   **Stillwater Intelligence:** The name of the robotic research team that finds facts for your video.
*   **Visual Assets:** The pictures or short video clips generated by the AI to play while the narrator is talking.
