# Chapter 1: Getting Started

> *Welcome to Video System — your AI-powered documentary studio.*

---

## What is Video System?

Think of Video System as a **one-person film crew** that lives in your browser. You bring the idea — a topic you're curious about — and the system handles everything else: the research, the writing, the visuals, the voiceover, the music, and even the upload to YouTube.

Imagine you want to make a documentary about the history of lighthouses. Normally, you'd need to spend days reading articles, writing a script, finding images, recording audio, and editing video. With Video System, you describe your topic and the AI handles each of these steps, one at a time, like stations on an assembly line.

---

## The Five Phases: Your Documentary Assembly Line

Every project in Video System moves through **five phases**, like a product moving down a factory line. Each phase builds on the last.

```
📝 Draft  →  🔬 Research  →  ✍️ Script  →  🎨 Visuals  →  🎬 Publish
```

Here's what happens at each station:

### Phase 1: Draft — "What's Your Topic?"

This is your starting point. You tell the system *what* you want to make a documentary about. Think of it like writing a title on a blank notebook.

**What you do:**
- Give your project a **title** (e.g., "The Secret Lives of Octopuses").
- Add a short **description** — a sentence or two about the angle you want to explore.
- Choose a **broad category** (Science, History, Technology, etc.).

**What happens next:**
The system creates a project card on your dashboard. Your project is now in the **Draft** phase — a blank canvas waiting for the research engine to fill it.

---

### Phase 2: Research — "Let the AI Do the Homework"

This is where the magic begins. When you click **"Open Stillwater Intelligence"**, a cinematic command center appears on screen. This is the **Stillwater Global Intelligence** panel — your AI research team.

**How it works — a simple analogy:**
Imagine sending five expert librarians into the world's biggest library. One specialises in history, another in science, a third in culture, and so on. They all search simultaneously and return with notes, quotes, and verified sources.

**What you do:**
1. **Choose a Persona** — This is like picking a "lens" for the research. Think of it as choosing which librarian leads the team:
   - 🕰️ **Historian**: Focuses on timelines and origins.
   - 🔬 **Scientist**: Looks at data and impact.
   - 🌍 **Culture Scout**: Searches for global context.
   - 🛡️ **Skeptic**: Double-checks everything critically.
   - 🔍 **Standard**: A balanced, all-purpose researcher.

2. **Click "Initialize Research Synthesis"** — The AI begins gathering facts, sources, and relevant data from across the internet.

3. **Watch the live feed** — You can see the researchers working in real time. Each one shows progress bars and status updates. If you accidentally close this window, look for the **"Live Intel"** button near the top of the page to reopen it.

**What you get:**
- A list of **verified sources** (articles, databases, academic papers).
- **Extracted facts** with confidence scores.
- A suggested **documentary outline** — the skeleton of your story.

> 💡 **Tip:** You don't need to understand any of the technical logs. They're there for transparency, but the system handles the complexity for you.

---

### Phase 3: Script — "From Facts to Story"

Once research is complete, the system turns your raw data into a **cinematic script** — a written narrative divided into sections, each with:

- **Voiceover text** — what the narrator will say.
- **Visual cues** — descriptions of what should appear on screen.
- **Timing estimates** — how long each section will last.

**Think of it like this:**
If research is gathering ingredients, scripting is the recipe. The AI takes all the facts and arranges them into a story that flows naturally — with an introduction, a build-up, key revelations, and a conclusion.

**What you do:**
- Click **"Generate Script"**.
- Review the sections. Each one has a title (like a chapter in a book).
- You can read through the script and make mental notes — or proceed directly to visuals.

> 💡 **Tip:** The system assigns a **Sleep-Friendliness Score** to your script. This measures how calming the pacing is — perfect if you're creating relaxation or bedtime content.

---

### Phase 4: Visuals — "Painting the Pictures"

Now the system generates **images and visual assets** that match your script. Each scene gets its own unique artwork, created by AI to fit the mood and topic.

**How it works:**
- The system reads each visual cue from your script (e.g., *"A lighthouse beam cutting through fog at dusk"*).
- It generates a high-quality image for each cue.
- You can choose a **Visual Style** to set the overall aesthetic:
  - 🎬 **Cinematic** — Dramatic, film-like visuals.
  - 🎨 **Oil Painting** — Classic, textured look.
  - 🌊 **Watercolor** — Soft and dreamy.
  - 🌸 **Studio Ghibli** — Animated, whimsical style.
  - ...and more.

**What you do:**
- Click **"Generate Visual Assets"**.
- Wait for the progress bar to complete (the system shows you which scene it's working on).
- Once finished, you'll see thumbnails of all generated images alongside your script.

---

### Phase 5: Assembly & Publishing — "Lights, Camera, Action!"

This is the final stretch. The system takes your script, voiceover audio, images, background music, and sound effects — and assembles them into a finished video.

**The assembly process has three sub-steps:**

1. **Assemble** — The system stitches everything together: narration, images, transitions, music, and ambient soundscapes. Think of it as the editor in a film studio putting all the clips in order.

2. **Render** — The assembled project is converted into a real video file (MP4). This is like pressing "Export" in a video editor. You'll see a progress bar showing which scene is being rendered.

3. **Publish** — Once your video is ready, you can:
   - **Download** the MP4 file to your computer.
   - **Publish directly to YouTube** — the system can connect to your YouTube account and upload automatically, complete with a title, description, and tags that are optimised for search engines.

## Your First Project: A Quick Walkthrough

Here's the fastest path from "nothing" to "documentary on YouTube":

| Step | What to Click | Time Estimate |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| 1 | Create a new project and enter your topic | 1 minute |
| 2 | Open Stillwater Intelligence → Pick a Persona → Launch Research | 2–3 minutes |
| 3 | Generate Script | 1–2 minutes |
| 4 | Choose a Visual Style → Generate Visual Assets | 3–5 minutes |
| 5 | Assemble → Render → Download or Publish | 5–10 minutes |

**Total estimated time: 12–21 minutes** from idea to finished documentary.

## Understanding the Status Bar

At the top of every project page, you'll see a large **status label** that tells you exactly where your project is in the assembly line:

| Status | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Draft** | Your project is a blank slate. Start research to move forward. |
| **Researching** | The AI is gathering facts. You can watch live or come back later. |
| **Scripting** | Your narrative is being generated from the research data. |
| **Generating Media** | Visual assets are being created for each scene. |
| **Assembling** | Audio, visuals, and music are being stitched together. |
| **Rendering** | The final video file is being produced. |
| **Ready** | Your documentary is complete! Download it or publish to YouTube. |
| **Published** | Your video is live on YouTube. 🎉 |

---

## What If Something Goes Wrong?

Don't worry — every step is reversible:

- **Closed the research window by accident?** Look for the glowing **"Live Intel"** badge next to the status title, or click **"VIEW LIVE INTELLIGENCE FEED"** in the action panel.
- **Want to redo visuals?** You can regenerate assets at any time without losing your script.
- **Not happy with the render?** Use **"Save Version & Re-edit"** to archive the current video and go back to the assembly phase.

> 💡 **Tip:** Your project auto-saves at every phase. You can close the browser and return later — your progress will be exactly where you left it.
