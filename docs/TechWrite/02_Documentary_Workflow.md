# Chapter 2: The Documentary Workflow

> *In this chapter, we will walk through the exact steps to build your first video from scratch. Think of this as the instruction manual for your factory.*

---

## Phase 1: How to Pick Your Subject (Project Initiation)

Every great documentary starts with an idea. In this phase, you are handing the blueprint to the factory.

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Click the **"New Project"** button on your dashboard.
2. Enter your **Topic**. This should be short and clear, like the title of a Wikipedia article (e.g., *The Building of the Pyramids* or *How Black Holes Work*).
3. Enter a **Description**. This tells the AI what specific parts of the topic you care about most. (e.g., *Focus on the tools they used and how many people it took.*)
4. Click **Save**.

**What happens next?** 
Your project is born! It is now in the **Draft** status. Your project gets a unique ID number, and a dashboard is created just for this video. 

> [!TIP]
> **A Note on SEO (Search Engine Optimization):** The system automatically looks at your topic and figures out if people are searching for it on YouTube. You don't have to do anything extra; the AI just uses this information to help title your video later!

---

## Phase 2: How to Gather Facts (Deep Research)

You wouldn't make a documentary without checking your facts. In this phase, we send the robotic librarians out into the real world. 

**Step-by-Step:**
1. On your project page, look for the big button that says **"Open Stillwater Intelligence"**. Click it.
2. Look for the **Persona** dropdown menu. Choose the "lens" you want to use (like *Historian* or *Scientist*).
3. Click the **"Initiate Research Synthesis"** button to start the process.
4. Watch the progress bars fill up. The terminal will show you when the AI finds facts and extracts quotes. 

> [!CAUTION]
> Do not close the browser while this happens, or you may interrupt the researchers. Grab a cup of coffee — it usually takes about 2 to 3 minutes.

---

## Phase 3: How to Write the Story (Cinematic Scripting)

Now you have a big pile of facts, quotes, and web links. It's time to turn that pile into a story someone can listen to. 

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Once the research is 100% complete, a new button will appear called **"Generate Script"**. Click it.
2. The AI will take all your facts and write a narrator's script. 
3. Scroll down to look at the script. It is broken into **Scenes** (like chapters in a book). Each scene will have the exact words the narrator will say. 

The AI isn't just writing words; it's also writing invisible instructions for the next step, deciding what pictures should appear on the screen while the narrator is talking.

---

## Phase 4: How to Create the Pictures (Visual Synthesis)

A documentary needs pictures! In this phase, the AI will paint high-quality images to match the script. 

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Look for the **"Visual Style"** dropdown. Let's pick *Cinematic* for a realistic, movie-like look, or *Watercolor* if you want it to look like a painting.
2. Click **"Generate Visual Assets"**. 
3. The system will go scene by scene, generating pictures based on the script's instructions. 
4. When it finishes, you'll see a small preview image next to every piece of text in your script.

> [!TIP]
> If a picture doesn't look quite right, you don't have to start over. Just click the small "Regenerate" button next to that specific picture to try again!

---

## Phase 5: How to Put It All Together (Assembly & Mastering)

This is the final step. The AI has the script, the voice, and the pictures. Now it's going to glue them all together, add background music, and create a real video file. 

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Look back at your project dashboard. 
2. Click the **"Assemble and Render"** button. 
3. The system will start building the video. It perfectly times the pictures to match the narrator's voice, and adds subtle sound effects. 
4. The status will change to **Rendering** as it builds the final MP4 file. 
5. When the status changes to **Ready**, you are done! 

**What happens next?**
You will see two new buttons on your screen:
*   **Download:** Saves the finished video file directly to your computer.
*   **Publish to YouTube:** Opens a window to send the video straight to your YouTube channel. The AI will even write the YouTube title and description for you. 

---

## Simple English Glossary

*   **Assembly Line / Render:** Sticking all the separate parts (audio, video, pictures) together into one final file you can watch on your phone.
*   **Persona:** The "personality" or "lens" the AI uses to look at a topic. 
*   **Stillwater Intelligence:** The name of the robotic research team that finds facts for your video.
*   **Visual Assets:** The pictures or short video clips generated by the AI to play while the narrator is talking.
