# Chapter 3: Core Features Guide

> *In this chapter, we'll explore the main tools in your documentary studio. You don't need to be a professional filmmaker to use them — Video System handles the heavy lifting.*

---

## 1. The Research Terminal: Watching the AI Work

When you start the Research phase, a black window with scrolling text will appear. This is the **Research Terminal** (often called *Stillwater Intelligence*). 

**What is it?** 
It's a live look at what your AI robotic librarians are doing in real time. 

**How to read it:**
You might see lines of text zooming past, saying things like "Querying Database" or "Extracting Facts." **You do not need to understand these messages.** 

Think of it like looking under the hood of a car while a mechanic is working. You can see the engine running, but you don't need to know how to build it. The terminal is just the system's way of saying, *"I'm working on it, please hold!"* 

**What you should look for:**
- **Progress Bars:** These show how close the research is to being finished.
- **Confidence Scores:** Sometimes you'll see a percentage (like 95%). This means the AI is 95% sure the fact it found is correct from multiple sources.

> [!TIP]
> If you close this window by accident, look for the flashing **"Live Intel"** button on your main project dashboard to bring it back.

## 2. Persona Lenses: Choosing Your Narrator's Voice

Before you send the robotic librarians out to research, you must choose a **Persona**. 

**What is it?**
A Persona is the "personality" or "angle" the AI will use to write your script. Think of it like hiring a specific type of expert to host your documentary.

**The Options:**
*   🕰️ **Historian:** Focuses on *when* and *where*. Great for biographies or explaining how things started.
*   🔬 **Scientist:** Focuses on *how* and *why*. Perfect for explaining weather, space, or technology. 
*   🌍 **Culture Scout:** Focuses on *people* and *society*. Best for videos about art, food, or traditions.
*   🛡️ **Skeptic:** Focuses on *truth* and *myths*. Ideal for debunking mysteries or fake news.
*   🔍 **Standard:** A balanced mix of everything. If you aren't sure which to pick, start here!

## 3. Director Suite: The Assembly Line Controller

Once your script and pictures are generated, you can view them all in the **Director Suite** (also called the *Timeline*). 

**What is it?**
This is where your movie comes together. It looks like a list of cards. Each card represents one scene in your documentary. 

**What you can do here:**
*   **See the Order:** The cards are stacked from top to bottom. The top card is the first thing the viewer will see.
*   **Check the Timing:** Each card shows how many seconds that scene will stay on the screen.
*   **Swap Pictures:** If you don't like picture the AI painted for Scene 2, you can ask it to paint a different one without changing the script.

Think of the Director Suite like a storyboard pinned to a wall. You can look at the whole movie at once before it gets finalized.

## 4. Audio Mixology: Setting the Mood

A good documentary needs more than just a voice talking; it needs an atmosphere. This is handled by **Audio Mixology**.

**What is it?**
It is the system that controls all the sounds in your video. 

**How it works:**
The system automatically layers three different types of sound:
1.  **The Voiceover:** The main narrator reading the script.
2.  **Background Music:** Soft music that plays underneath the voice.
3.  **Ambiance (Sound Effects):** Subtle noises that match the picture. If your picture shows a rainy forest, the system might add the sound of raindrops.

**The best part?** You don't have to fiddle with volume knobs. The AI uses "Auto-Ducking." This is a fancy term that means the background music automatically gets quieter when the narrator is speaking, and gets louder when they take a breath.

## 5. The Sleep-Friendliness Engine: Making Relaxing Videos

Many people watch documentaries to help them fall asleep. Video System has a special tool just for this called the **Sleep-Friendliness Engine**.

**What is it?**
It is a built-in critic that scores how relaxing your video is, from 0 to 100. 

**How it scores your video:**
*   **Pacing:** Are the sentences short and calm, or fast and loud?
*   **Colors:** Did the AI paint using soft, dark colors (good for sleep), or bright, flashing neon colors (bad for sleep)?
*   **Volume:** Is the background music too loud compared to the narrator?

If you want to make a video specifically for bedtime, you can tell the AI to prioritize a high Sleep-Friendliness score. It will automatically choose softer voices, darker images, and slower music.

## Simple English Glossary

*   **Ambiance:** Background noises that make a scene feel real (like wind blowing or birds chirping).
*   **Auto-Ducking:** When the music automatically lowers its volume so you can hear the narrator talk.
*   **Timeline:** The order of your scenes from start to finish. 
*   **Terminal:** A screen that shows the computer "thinking" in real-time.
